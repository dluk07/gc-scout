Glossary

Term What it means
Power rating (Massey) Overall strength in runs vs. an average team in this pool. +3 ≈ three runs better than average. Solved by least-squares over every game margin.
Elo Independent strength score (starts at 1500) updated game-by-game; a cross-check on Massey. Higher = stronger; ~100 pts ≈ a meaningful gap.
Offense (adj) Opponent-adjusted runs scored vs. average. +1.5 means they score ~1.5 runs more than a typical team would against the same defenses.
Defense (adj) Opponent-adjusted runs prevented vs. average. Positive = good defense (they allow fewer runs than a typical team would vs. the same offenses).
runs scored/allowed per game Raw, unadjusted averages — easy to read but flattered or punished by schedule strength.
Strength of schedule (SoS) Average power rating of the opponents a team has faced. Amber = tough slate, blue = soft slate.
Projected runs / win prob Expected final margin and win chance for your matchup, blending the rating gap, common opponents, and any head-to-head.
Common opponents Teams both you and the opponent have played; comparing your margins vs. theirs controls for schedule.
Head-to-head (h2h) Games you've played against that opponent directly.
Confidence How much data backs the projection (common-opponent count + h2h + games played).
Home-field Estimated run value of playing at home, from the model.

Notes & caveats

  • Ratings come from a Massey least-squares model on game margins (home-field + ridge regularization); Elo is a chronological cross-check.
  • Offense/Defense ratings are a separate opponent-adjusted least-squares split, so facing weak teams inflates raw runs but not the adjusted numbers.
  • Only seed teams (ids you passed) have full schedules; other teams appear only via games against the seeds, so their ratings are based on partial data.
  • Youth results are noisy and team-name matching across feeds is fuzzy — treat projections as directional. Confidence reflects common-opponent count + head-to-head.
  • To deepen strength-of-schedule, add more team ids as seeds (see the unresolved opponents listed by main.py).
